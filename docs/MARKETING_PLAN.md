# Fieldmark Marketing Plan

Version 1.0 — May 2026

---

## Strategic Foundation

**The One Thing:**

Farmers are making $100,000 decisions in the dark. Fieldmark changes that.

**The Urgency Reality:**

There is one peak conversion window per year — January and February, when farmers commit to March input purchases. Every marketing activity builds toward that window.

**The Distribution Reality:**

Starting cold with a modest budget means zero paid acquisition in Year 1. Every dollar goes toward relationship-building, content that compounds, and co-op partnership development. Word of mouth from one trusted farmer is worth more than $5,000 in Facebook ads to this audience.

**The Core Marketing Insight:**

Farmers don't buy software. They buy confidence from people they trust. The marketing job is not to sell Fieldmark — it is to get Fieldmark in front of the trusted people who influence farmers, and let the product do the selling.

**Year 1 Goal:**

40–60 paying farmers at $150/month → $6,000–$9,000 MRR by February 2027

## Target Audiences (In Priority Order)

### Primary: The Farmer

Mid-scale commodity farmer

- 500–5,000 acres, corn and/or soybean
- Midwest, primarily Missouri
- Age 40–60, digitally capable
- Currently spending $200–300/month on fragmented tools that don't answer the core question

**Where they are:**

- Co-op pre-season meetings
- Extension office events
- Facebook (farm groups, not ads)
- LinkedIn (less active but present)
- Farm shows and county fairs
- Coffee shop conversations at the co-op

**What they respond to:**

- Specific dollar amounts ("$103,400 above average")
- Peer validation ("farms like yours in your county")
- Independent credibility (MU Extension data)
- No-risk entry (30-day free trial)

### Secondary: The Agronomist / Co-op Rep

The trusted advisor with the most influence — 35–45% of farmer input decisions. Will recommend Fieldmark if it makes them look good and doesn't threaten their role.

**The pitch to them:**

> We make your farmers more financially disciplined. Better conversations. More confident clients. We work through you, not around you.

### Tertiary: The Ag Lender

35–40% influence on farmer decisions. Wants borrowers who understand their margins. Will recommend Fieldmark to reduce default risk.

**The pitch to them:**

> Your borrowers show up with better data. You spend less time on risky loans. We make your portfolio healthier.

## 60-Day Plan — Traction (July 2026)

### Goal

First co-op conversation. First case study. 10–15 farmers using the app.

### Co-op Outreach (Start Day 31)

**The Approach**

Do not cold pitch the software. Lead with the data.

**Email template for co-op general managers:**

**Subject:** MU Extension benchmark data + your member farmers

> [Name],
>
> I'm building a financial planning tool for mid-scale corn and soybean farmers in Missouri that uses MU Extension 2026 crop budgets as the independent benchmark baseline.
>
> I'm reaching out to [Co-op Name] because I'd like to understand how your agronomists currently help members think through input cost decisions before March commitments.
>
> Would you have 20 minutes for a phone call? I'm not asking you to promote anything — I'm trying to understand how co-ops like yours approach pre-season financial planning with members, and whether there's a fit worth exploring.
>
> Jeff Gipson  
> Fieldmark  
> fieldmark.app

**What you're listening for on that call:**

- How do they currently help farmers with financial planning?
- Do they have a pre-season meeting schedule?
- Would they be interested in a data partnership vs a distribution partnership?
- Who is the lead agronomist you should meet?

**Goal:** 3 co-op calls completed by Day 60

### Extension Office Outreach

Missouri Extension has offices in every county. The Cape Girardeau County Extension office is your first target — you're local.

Find: Commercial Agriculture Field Specialist (MU Extension has these specifically for corn/soy farm financial planning)

Email approach similar to co-op but lead with:

> I built a consumer-facing tool that uses your published 2026 crop budgets as the benchmark baseline. I credit MU Extension on every benchmark comparison the farmer sees.

Extension agents love seeing their data used correctly. This is a warm opening.

**Goal:** 1 extension agent conversation by Day 60

### First Case Study

By Day 60 you should have at least one farmer who has been using the app for 30 days.

Interview them. Ask:

- What did you find out that surprised you?
- Did you change any input decisions?
- Have you shared the report with your lender?
- What would you tell another farmer about it?

Write it up as a one-page story. Real farm name only with permission. Otherwise: "A 400-acre corn farmer in Cape Girardeau County."

Use specific numbers. Example: "His fertilizer cost was $47/acre above the regional average. After seeing that, he negotiated a different purchase timing and reduced his total fertilizer spend by $14,000."

This case study goes:

- On the website
- In co-op outreach emails
- In LinkedIn posts
- In every sales conversation going forward

### Content: Double Down on What Works

By Day 45 you'll know which LinkedIn posts got traction. Double down on that format.

Introduce a weekly series: **"Before March" — one data point per week**

Examples:

- "Missouri corn farmers paid an average of $187/ac for fertilizer in 2026 according to MU Extension. How does your cost compare?"
- "The breakeven price for a Central Missouri corn farm with average costs is $2.23/bu. At current futures prices, that's $2.10 of margin per bushel. Run your downside."
- "8 months. That's how long a Missouri corn farmer carries input costs before harvest revenue arrives. At 5% interest on $120,000 in operating costs, that's $6,000 in carrying cost before you sell a bushel."

These are shareable, educational, and drive farmers to wonder where they stand.

### Email List Setup

Start collecting emails now. Use a simple lead magnet:

**"2026 Missouri Corn & Soybean Input Cost Benchmark Guide"**

One-page PDF. Free download. Actual MU Extension 2026 numbers formatted cleanly with regional breakdowns.

Put it on the website:

> Download the 2026 Missouri crop budget benchmarks — the same independent data Fieldmark uses.

Every download = email address. Every email address = future customer.

**Email sequence after download:**

| Day | Email |
|-----|-------|
| 0 | Deliver the PDF |
| 3 | "Here's what the data means for March" |
| 7 | "How your costs compare — a quick tool" |
| 14 | "30 days free — no credit card" |

## 90-Day Plan — Momentum (August 2026)

### Goal

First co-op MOU signed or in negotiation. 20–30 farmers using the app. First lender conversation. Pre-season content calendar built for fall.

### Co-op Partnership Development

By Day 90 you want one co-op to say: "We'd like to pilot this with our members at our pre-season meeting."

Pre-season meetings happen September–November. You need a signed MOU by end of September to be included in their fall planning cycle.

**The Co-op Pitch (for the follow-up meeting)**

Frame it as three things:

1. **Data partnership (not distribution)** — "Your cost data is already in your systems. If you share it with us via CSV export, your members see their costs compared to their actual co-op peers — not just MU Extension averages. That's more valuable benchmarking for your members."

2. **Advisor enablement (not replacement)** — "Your agronomists stay central. Farmers use Fieldmark to prepare for conversations with your agronomists — they show up more informed and ask better questions. That's a better member relationship for you."

3. **Revenue share (aligned incentives)** — "For every farmer you refer who becomes a paying subscriber, you receive 40% of their monthly subscription revenue. At $150/month, that's $60/month per referred farmer. At 50 referred farmers, that's $3,000/month in passive revenue for your co-op."

**The MOU Terms to Offer:**

- 60/40 revenue share (farmer to co-op)
- White-label option (co-op branding on reports)
- Co-op gets aggregate benchmark data from their member farmers
- Fieldmark handles all technology and support
- Co-op provides one agronomist for onboarding calls during pilot

### Ag Lender Outreach

Target: Farm Credit Southeast Missouri, local community banks with ag portfolios

**The pitch:**

> Your borrowers show up to annual review meetings with better financial data. They've already modeled their downside. They know their cost position vs peers. That makes your underwriting easier and your portfolio healthier.

**Offer:** White-label lender report. "The lender report D.A.L.E. generates can be branded with your institution's logo and shared with you directly by the farmer."

**Goal:** 1 lender conversation by Day 90

### Pre-Season Content Calendar (Build in August)

September through December is your content push season. Build it all in August so you're not scrambling.

| Month | Theme | Volume | Focus |
|-------|-------|--------|-------|
| September | Know before you commit | 4 LinkedIn, 8 Facebook | The March problem, the information gap |
| October | What your peers are paying | 4 LinkedIn, 8 Facebook | Benchmark data, peer comparison reveals |
| November | Model your downside | 4 LinkedIn, 8 Facebook | Scenario modeling, worst-case planning |
| December | Walk into March ready | 4 LinkedIn, 8 Facebook | The full Fieldmark workflow, free trial push |

Missouri has several fall farm shows and extension field days:

Submit to speak on: "Using independent benchmark data to validate input cost decisions before March"

**Goal:** 1 speaking submission by Day 90

## Six-Month Plan — Scale (September–November 2026)

### Goal

Co-op pilot running with 20–50 farmers. 50+ farmers on email list. Speaking at one extension event. Lender pilot conversation active. Pipeline for January/February launch push.

### September: Co-op Pilot Launch

If MOU is signed, co-op agronomists begin recommending Fieldmark at pre-season meetings.

**Agronomist training kit:**

- One-pager: "What Fieldmark does for your members"
- Talking points: How to introduce it
- Demo credentials: So they can show it live
- FAQ: What farmers will ask

**Farmer onboarding via co-op:**

1. Agronomist introduces Fieldmark
2. Farmer signs up for 30-day free trial
3. Agronomist does one 30-minute onboarding call alongside Fieldmark setup
4. Farmer runs first scenario with agronomist present

This is the moment the product sells itself. An agronomist who watches a farmer discover their fertilizer cost is 23% above average becomes your best salesperson.

### October: Content Push + Email Nurture

Weekly email to list:

| Week | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 1 | Pre-season planning starts now |
| 2 | 2026 Missouri benchmark data — what it shows for your operation |
| 3 | Modeling your March decision |
| 4 | What to bring to your lender meeting |

Each email has one CTA: Start free trial.

### November: Conversion Push

Black Friday angle for ag audience doesn't work. Instead: **"Before the December lull"**

> January is coming fast. Your agronomist will call in February. Your lender meeting is in January. Start your 30-day trial now and have your analysis ready.

Launch the email sequence hard in November. Every farmer on your list gets a direct personal-feeling email from Jeff:

> Hey [Name] — I wanted to reach out personally. You downloaded our benchmark guide back in [month]. March is 3 months away. I'd like to give you 30 days free access to see where Henderson Family Farm-size operations in your county actually stand. No credit card. No commitment. Just your numbers vs your peers. Reply and I'll set it up personally.

Personal email from the founder converts dramatically better than automation at this stage.

### December: Pipeline for January Launch

By December 31 you want:

- 30-day free trial invites sent to every warm contact
- Co-op pilot farmers mid-trial
- 3+ case study farmers identified
- Lender pilot conversation active
- Speaking confirmed for January event if possible

## January–February 2027 — The March Window

This is the Super Bowl. Everything builds to this.

### January: Full Conversion Push

**Paid promotion — first time spending on ads**

- Budget: $500–$1,000
- Platform: Facebook only
- Audience: Missouri, 35–60, farming interests, farm equipment pages, MFA co-op followers
- Ad format: Video (use your demo video)
- Message: "Know your margins before March. 30 days free. fieldmark.app"
- Run for 4 weeks in January only. Stop in February — organic word of mouth takes over if the product is working.

**Co-op pre-season meeting presence**

Be at every co-op meeting you can get into. Not as a vendor — as the guy with the MU Extension benchmark data.

> I can show you how your costs compare to other farms in this county.

That's your opening line.

**PR push**

Pitch these outlets:

- Missouri Farmer Today
- Missouri Ruralist
- Southeast Missourian (local)
- MU Extension news releases

**Story angle:**

> Cape Girardeau startup gives farmers independent data to counter advisor conflicts of interest before March

That story writes itself. A local startup using MU Extension data to help local farmers is exactly what regional ag media covers.

### February: Retention Activation

**Every trial farmer who hasn't converted:** Personal phone call or text from Jeff.

> Did you get a chance to run your scenario? I'd love to walk you through it — 15 minutes.

**Every converted farmer:**

> Share this with one farmer you know.

Word of mouth referral is your best CAC at this stage.
