# Fieldmark Brand Guide

## Brand Foundation

**Product Name:** Fieldmark

**Tagline:** Know your margins before March.

**Mission:** Give mid-scale Midwest corn and soybean farmers the independent financial data they need to validate input decisions before committing capital.

**Vision:** A future where farmers walk into every March input decision with peer-benchmarked clarity — shifting from anxiety to confidence.

**Brand Promise:** Fieldmark gives you the independent data you need to know where you stand before March.

## Brand Personality

**Archetype:** The Mentor

**Personality Traits:**
- Pragmatic — focused on real problems and concrete numbers
- Direct — plainspoken Midwest language, no jargon or hype
- Trustworthy — honest about limits, backs claims with data
- Independent — no relationship with vendors, co-ops, or agronomists

**If Fieldmark were a person:**
They would be an experienced ag finance advisor who respects your expertise, shows you data you don't have access to, and helps you make decisions with confidence. They would never hype features, oversimplify your business, or claim to replace your trusted advisors.

## Mascot — Dale the Owl

Dale is Fieldmark's AI agricultural financial analyst. He is represented by a barn owl mascot character.

**Why an owl:**
- Wisdom — the right metaphor for independent analysis
- Sees in the dark — Dale sees what others can't or won't show you
- Directly connects to the tagline "See what they see"
- Native to Missouri farmland — authentic, not manufactured

**Dale's identity:**
- Completely independent — no financial relationship with any input vendor, seed company, co-op, or agronomist
- Plainspoken — Midwest language, specific numbers, no jargon
- Proactive — leads with findings, doesn't wait to be asked
- Honest — always shows downside scenarios, never guarantees outcomes
- Respectful — never patronizes farmers or explains what they know

**Dale's image assets:**
- `dale-avatar.png` — teal circle head, chat bubbles and navigation
- `dale-sitting.png` — sitting with checklist, empty states
- `dale-standing.png` — standing pointing, onboarding screens
- `dale-analyzing.png` — holding financial report, results screens
- `dale-waving.png` — waving, welcome and success screens

Assets live at `client/public/images/dale/`.

**Export requirements:** Use true PNG with an alpha channel (transparent background). Do not export as JPEG or PNG with a baked-in black/white backdrop — the app uses cream (`#F5F3F0`) page backgrounds.

**Dale's disclaimer (always shown somewhere near his output):**
> Dale's analysis is generated by AI using your farm data and University of Missouri Extension benchmarks. It is for planning purposes and does not constitute financial advice.

### Where Dale appears (product vs mascot)

**Fieldmark** is the product brand on public pages — independent data, benchmarks, margins. **Dale** is the in-app analyst; he should not dominate marketing the way a consumer mascot would.

| Surface | Dale presence | Rationale |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Landing (`/`) | None in hero; small avatar only on the “Talk to Dale” feature card | Brand guide: landing is problem-first, not Dale-led |
| Login | None — Fieldmark logo only | Utility screen; farmer already chose the product |
| Register | Optional medium `waving` | Onboarding — warm welcome into the product |
| Inside app (dashboard, chat, benchmarks, reports) | Full — briefing cards, sidebar avatar, chat, analyzing/sitting variants | Dale-led experience after sign-in |
| Reports (print) | Small avatar in header | Identifies analyst without cartoon-heavy lender doc |

Do not use Dale as the primary hero on public pages. Lead with tagline, MU benchmarks, and margin story; introduce Dale as one capability among three (benchmark, scenario, analyst).

### UI direction (modern, not “AI product”)

**Goal:** Calm B2B fintech — credible for lenders and farmers. Avoid generic AI SaaS tropes.

| Do | Avoid |
|----|--------|
| Cream canvas + white elevated panels | Purple/blue gradients, mesh blobs, glassmorphism |
| Teal for action only; data in Montserrat tabular nums | “AI-powered” badges, sparkle icons, chat as homepage hero |
| Subtle field-grid texture, soft shadows | Neon glows, dark mode for marketing pages |
| Progressive disclosure; numbers lead | Cartoon mascot dominating every screen |
| Dale as analyst *inside* the product | Dale as the public brand face |

**Implementation:** `client/src/index.css` (`.fm-canvas`, `.fm-panel`, `.fm-card`, `.fm-eyebrow`, `.fm-stat`).

### Color Rules
- Never use pure black (`#000000`) on pure white (`#FFFFFF`) — too harsh
- Always use Warm Charcoal (`#4A4A4A`) for body text on Cream backgrounds
- Teal is for action — buttons, links, active states only
- Gold is for attention — Dale alerts, flagged costs, important callouts
- Red is for danger — negative numbers, risk flags, critical warnings
- Green is for strength — below average costs, positive margins

## Typography

### Font Stack

**Display / Headlines: Montserrat**
- Source: Google Fonts
- Weights: 600 (SemiBold), 700 (Bold)
- Use: Page titles, section headers, hero text, card headings
- Feel: Confident, clear, professional without being aggressive

**Body / UI: Lato**
- Source: Google Fonts
- Weights: 400 (Regular), 700 (Bold)
- Use: Body text, labels, form fields, table data, captions
- Feel: Clean, readable on screen, friendly without being casual

### Type Scale

| Name | Size | Weight | Font | Use |
|------|------|--------|------|-----|
| Hero | 48px | 700 | Montserrat | Landing page headline |
| H1 | 36px | 700 | Montserrat | Page titles |
| H2 | 28px | 600 | Montserrat | Section headers |
| H3 | 22px | 600 | Montserrat | Card titles, subsections |
| H4 | 18px | 600 | Montserrat | Table headers, labels |
| Body Large | 18px | 400 | Lato | Lead paragraphs, Dale messages |
| Body | 16px | 400 | Lato | Standard body text |
| Body Small | 14px | 400 | Lato | Captions, timestamps, footnotes |
| Label | 12px | 700 | Lato | Form labels, tags, badges (uppercase, 0.5px letter-spacing) |
| Data | 32px | 700 | Montserrat | Key metrics, big numbers |

### Typography Rules
- Minimum body text: 16px
- Line height body: 1.6
- Line height headings: 1.2
- Never more than 4 type sizes on one screen
- Data numbers (margins, costs, percentiles) always Montserrat Bold
- Never use italic for data — use weight and color instead

## Voice and Tone

**Use these words:**
know, margin, scenario, peer, spend, decision, validate, benchmark, ammunition, independent, confidence, per acre, total farm, downside, carrying cost, regional average

**Never use:**
empower, transform, revolutionary, innovative, disrupt, leverage, game-changing, cutting-edge, AI-powered, synergy, seamless, unlock, supercharge

**Tone by screen:**

| Screen | Tone |
|--------|------|
| Landing page | Direct, problem-first, specific |
| Onboarding | Warm, guiding, step-by-step |
| Dashboard | Informational, Dale-led, findings-first |
| Benchmark reveal | Factual, specific, let numbers speak |
| Dale chat | Conversational, Midwest plain, direct |
| Reports | Professional, citable, lender-appropriate |

**Numbers always:**
- Show dollar signs: `$187/acre` not `187/acre`
- Show direction: ↑ above average, ✓ below average
- Show total farm impact: not just per acre
- Show the comparison: Your cost vs regional average
